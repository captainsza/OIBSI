Biography

Nikola Tesla (1856â€“1943) was a Serbian-American inventor, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and futurist who made significant contributions to the development of the alternating current (AC) electrical system.

His work in AC power transmission and radio communication paved the way for many of the technological advances of the 20th century. Tesla held over 300 patents and is best known for his invention of the Tesla coil and contributions to the development of the modern electric power system.

Tesla's groundbreaking ideas and inventions continue to influence science and technology to this day. He remains an iconic figure in the history of electrical engineering and innovation.

Key Inventions

Tesla Coil: Tesla's invention of the Tesla coil was fundamental in the development of wireless transmission of electricity and radio communication.

AC Motor: Tesla's alternating current (AC) motor design revolutionized the power industry and remains widely used today.

Radio: Tesla made pioneering contributions to the development of radio technology, although credit is often given to others.

Legacy

Tesla's Legacy Lives On

Nikola Tesla's contributions to science and technology continue to shape our world:

  • AC power transmission is the standard for electricity distribution worldwide.
  • His concepts inspired innovations in wireless communication and robotics.
  • The SI unit of magnetic flux density, the tesla (T), is named in his honor.
